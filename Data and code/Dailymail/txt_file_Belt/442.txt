Patrick Stewart takes a victory lap at Vegas Star Trek convention after announcing he'll reprise role of Jean-Luc Picard
https://www.dailymail.co.uk/tvshowbiz/article-6028045/Patrick-Stewart-visits-Vegas-convention-announcing-hell-reprise-role-Jean-Luc-Picard.html
 07:16 BST, 5 August 2018 
Patrick Stewart came up a big winner in Vegas Saturday.The 78-year-old English actor, who announced he will reprise the role of character Jean-Luc Picard in a new Star Trek series on CBS All Access, soaked in the cheers from the crowd at the Rio Hotel & Casino for Creation Entertainment's 2018 Star Trek Convention Las Vegas.Stewart, who was a surprise guest, wore a white T-shirt tucked into blue jeans with a belt as he hailed the crowd at the Sin City venue.         In his element: Patrick Stewart, 78, soaked in the cheers from a crowd at the Rio Hotel & Casino for Creation Entertainment's 2018 Star Trek Convention Las Vegas Saturday, which came alongside his revelation he'll play Captain Jean-Luc Picard on a new Star Trek series The veteran actor last played the iconic Star Trek character in the 2002 film Star Trek: Nemesis, which was part of the Star Trek: The Next Generation universe.Also on hand was another legend of the franchise, William Shatner, 87, who wore a navy blue button-up shirt over a black V-neck top.  Stewart announced his return via a Twitter statement on Saturday. His statement was in two parts, consisting of a tweet and an image containing a much longer explanation.         Primed for action: The English entertainer said he was 'excited and invigorated' to take the role back on                Keeping it simple: The beloved actor wore a white T-shirt and blue jeans on the summer day in the desert          You can go home again: Stewart said on social media he originally thought he was done playing Picard 16 years ago, but has since had a change of heart 'It is an unexpected but delightful surprise to find myself excited and invigorated to be returning to Jean-Luc Picard and to explore new dimensions within him,' he excitedly announced in the tweet.Alongside a black and white portrait of himself, he went on to explain the thought process behind his decision to assume the role once again.'I will always be very proud to have been a part of Star Trek: The Next Generation, but when we wrapped that final movie in the spring of 2002, I truly felt my time with Star Trek had run its natural course,' he admitted initially.          Focused: Stewart revealed that a combination of personal experiences and the current political climate pushed him to pursue Jean-Luc once again         Familiar face: William Shatner, who played Captain James T. Kirk on the series, also appeared before fans at the desert venue                 Icon: The veteran actor appeared on the original series during its 1966-1969 run, in addition to multiple films that followed in the years after But then he revealed that a combination of personal experiences and the current political climate pushed him to pursue Jean-Luc once again. 'During these past years, it has been humbling to hear many stories about how The Next Generation brought people comfort, saw them through difficult periods in their lives or how the example of Jean-Luc inspired so many to follow in his footsteps, pursuing science, exploration and leadership,' he said farther down in the statement.He added: 'I feel I’m ready to return to him for the same reason – to research and experience what comforting and reforming light he might shine on these often very dark times. I look forward to working with our brilliant creative team as we endeavor to bring a fresh, unexpected and pertinent story to life once more.'         Shine a light: Stewart said he hopes to shine a comforting and reforming light 'on these often very dark times' in playing the role again         Exciting!
