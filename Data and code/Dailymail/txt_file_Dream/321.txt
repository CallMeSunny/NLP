Obama, Xi agree to step up nuclear safety cooperation -Chinese official By Reuters   Published: 22:06 BST, 31 March 2016  |  Updated: 22:06 BST, 31 March 2016                e-mail         WASHINGTON, March 31 (Reuters) - U.S. President Barack Obama and Chinese President Xi Jinping, meeting on the sidelines of a nuclear summit, agreed on Thursday to step up international nuclear security cooperation and uphold the international non-proliferation regime, a senior Chinese official said."The presidents of the two countries had candid and in-depth exchange of views on a variety of issues ... and reached an important consensus. The meeting was positive, constructive and fruitful," Zheng Zeguang, China's assistant foreign minister, told reporters. (Reporting by David Brunnstrom and Lesley Wroughton; Editing by James Dalgleish)
