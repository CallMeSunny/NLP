Obama says reached 'common understanding' with Xi on cyber By Reuters   Published: 18:43 BST, 25 September 2015  |  Updated: 18:43 BST, 25 September 2015                e-mail         WASHINGTON, Sept 25 (Reuters) - U.S. President Barack Obama on Friday said he and Chinese President Xi Jinping reached a "common understanding" on addressing cyber spying issues and agreed that neither would conduct or knowingly support cyber-enabled theft of intellectual property.Obama said he raised "serious concerns" about growing cyber threats with Xi. "I indicated that it has to stop," he said, though he also urged further cooperation."This is progress, but I have to insist that our work is not yet done." (Reporting by Julia Edwards, Roberta Rampton and Jeff Mason; Writing by Alina Selyukh; Editing by Doina Chiacu)
