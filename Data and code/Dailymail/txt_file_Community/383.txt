One win from greatness: Golden State Warriors set up a date with destiny as they tie Michael Jordan's Chicago Bulls for most victories in a season 
https://www.dailymail.co.uk/news/article-3533413/Golden-State-Warriors-tie-Michael-Jordan-s-Chicago-Bulls-victories-season.html
 07:04 BST, 11 April 2016 
The Golden State Warriors got an historic victory in on Sunday for their 72nd win, tying the 1995-96 Chicago Bulls for the record season wins.Stephen Curry had 37 points and the Warriors beat the Spurs 92-86 in San Antonio, where they hadn't won in nearly two decades.The Warriors will finish their regular season at home against the Memphis Grizzlies on Wednesday night.         The Golden State Warriors celebrated their 72nd win on Sunday, tying the 1995-96 Chicago Bulls for the record season wins         The Warriors beat the Spurs 92-86 in San Antonio, where they hadn't won in nearly two decades. Pictured above, Spurs' Manu Ginobili, bottom, of Argentina, chases the ball against Golden State Warriors' Harrison Barnes during the first half       A win on Wednesday would break the current season record held by the 1995-96 Chicago Bulls, a team that included Michael Jordan (pictured in 1990), Dennis Rodman and Scottie Pippen         Also on the 1995-96 Bulls roster was Steve Kerr (right), who is the current head coach of the Golden State WarriorsA win on Wednesday would break the current season record held by the 1995-96 Chicago Bulls, a team that included Michael Jordan, Dennis Rodman and Scottie Pippen.Also on the team was Steve Kerr, the current head coach of the Golden State Warriors.Prior to Sunday's game, the Spurs had won 48 consecutive regular-season games at home, including an NBA-record 39 straight to begin this season. Golden State ended a 33-game skid in San Antonio that dated to 1997.  San Antonio sat Tim Duncan for rest and was without an injured Boris Diaw. It had not lost at home since March 2015.The Warriors dominated the fourth quarter. Curry controlled the action with a series of layups when San Antonio's defense attempted to run him off the 3-point line.In their lone victory over Golden State this season, the Spurs managed to slow the game down.          Golden State Warriors shooting guard Klay Thompson (11) dribbles around San Antonio Spurs point guard Tony Parker (9, left)         Golden State Warriors forward Draymond Green (23) reacts to a foul call as Warriors guard Stephen Curry talks to him during the first halfSan Antonio tried to match the Warriors' pace on Sunday, though, leading to a tense and at times sloppy game.LaMarcus Aldridge had 24 points to lead the Spurs, who have lost three straight for the first time this season. Kawhi Leonard added 20 points but shot 7 for 22 from the field while continually being harried by Draymond Green.Green got a technical for charging directly toward Monty McCutcheon at the scorer's table after the official ruled he fouled Leonard in the first half. With Andrew Bogut screaming from the Warriors' bench for teammates to grab Green, the Warriors' energetic forward followed McCutcheon across the court to continue his argument. A second technical was not issued.Green finished with 11 points and Klay Thompson added 14 for Golden State.The Warriors will host the Memphis Grizzlies on Wednesday and attempt to break the season win record.           Golden State Warriors forward Andre Iguodala, right, passes around Kawhi Leonard during the first half of Sunday's game         Stephen Curry (30) celebrates with Draymond Green (23) of the Golden State Warriors during a game against the San Antonio Spurs         Golden State Warriors' Stephen Curry, right, and Draymond Green, celebrate the win. The Warriors will host the Memphis Grizzlies on Wednesday and attempt to break the season win record
