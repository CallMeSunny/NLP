Obama, Xi spar over pro-democracy protests in Hong Kong By Reuters   Published: 05:22 BST, 12 November 2014  |  Updated: 05:22 BST, 12 November 2014                e-mail         BEIJING, Nov 12 (Reuters) - U.S. President Barack Obama and Chinese President Xi Jinping sparred on Wednesday over the pro-democracy protests in Hong Kong, with Obama saying the U.S. wanted to encourage free and fair elections and Xi saying the issues in the territory were an internal Chinese affair.The two made the comments during a news conference following talks at Beijing's Great Hall of the People. (Reporting by Matt Spetalnick and Michael Martina; Writing by Ben Blanchard; Editing by Raju Gopalakrishnan)
