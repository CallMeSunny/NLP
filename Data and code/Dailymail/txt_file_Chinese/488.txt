Chinese leader will NOT golf with Trump at Mar-a-Lago says White House as it says visit will feature meals and 'informal' talks
https://www.dailymail.co.uk/news/article-4380488/Trump-rolling-red-carpet-Chinese-president-Xi.html
 23:16 BST, 4 April 2017 
President Donald Trump's Mar-a-Lago summit with China's Xi Jinping will not involve 'golf clubs.'A casual dinner the first evening, a working lunch the next day and the opportunity for other 'informal interactions' were on the table, a senior administration official said, and the presidents' wives will also be there. 'I think it's safe to say there's not gonna be any golf,' the official said of the two-day, getting-to-know you trip to Florida that commences on Thursday.         President Donald Trump's Mar-a-Lago summit with China's Xi Jinping will not involve 'golf clubs'Trump wooed Japanese Prime Minister Shinzo Abe over golf during a February retreat at his private club. But Xi does not play golf. He's waging a war against the sport in China and considers it a symbol of government corruption.Barack Obama met with Xi at a golf resort, too, when the Chinese leader came to visit him in 2013. The pair held talks at Sunnylands in Rancho Mirage, California.  The White House suggested Monday that it was Xi's idea to meet in Florida. An official backtracked Tuesday and said that it was Trump who extended the invitation to his Chinese counterpart.Trump and Xi are seeing each other for the first time this week as the two leaders begin to build a framework for in-depth talks on trade, the South China Sea and North Korea. 'This is an opportunity for President Trump to develop a relationship in person with President Xi,' White House Press Secretary Sean Spicer said ahead of the trip. 'There are big issues of national and economic security that need to get addressed, and I think there’s going to be a lot on the table when it comes to that over the two days that they will talk.'The agenda for talks remained fluid on Tuesday, with a senior administration official saying the two leaders would only just be beginning their working relationship.'I don't anticipate that there will be a resolution on those issues,' the official said. The Chinese leader is seen here with his wife todayThe topic of North Korean aggression was 'certainly' going to come up, the official said. The White House refused to broadcast the president's tone and remarks on that and other expected subjects, however, while stating that Trump would be clear with Xi about how important it is. The official, who held the briefing for reporters on behalf of the White House, would not comment on the president's plans to bring up currency manipulation - a topic he's railed on before.In terms of Taiwan, the official said Trump would reaffirm the United States' one-China policy.'That is longstanding policy of the United States. I don't anticipate some kind of surprising deviation from that.'During the briefing the official said that Trump invited Xi to Palm Beach to the 'Winter White House' as Mar-a-Lago is now being called.'I'm certain it was President Trump's invitation that they meet outside of Washington D.C., and this is the first time that the two are going to be meeting,' the official said. 'It's a place where he feels comfortable and at home and where he can break the ice with Xi Jinping without the formality' of Washington.White House Press Secretary Sean Spicer had indicated the day before that it was the Chinese who wanted to meet outside the White House in his daily briefing.'The request to go to Mar-a-Lago was something that the Chinese,' he said, stopping himself, then stating that it was 'negotiated with the Chinese.''I think this is a very high-level visit that really has a huge impact on both economic and national security,' he added.
