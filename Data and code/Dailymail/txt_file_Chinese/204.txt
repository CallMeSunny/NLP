For a lucky Lunar New Year! Chinese fortune-seekers around the world flock to light incense in temples to celebrate the first day of the Year of the Dog
https://www.dailymail.co.uk/news/article-5399207/Chinese-people-rush-pray-day-Lunar-New-Year.html
 10:55 BST, 16 February 2018 
Chinese people around the world are heading to temples today to celebrate the arrival of the Lunar New Year.Fortune-seekers light joss sticks in holy shrines to pray for happiness, prosperity and longevity as they welcome the Year of the Dog.In China, dedicated prayers queued up outside of temples from the eve of the Lunar New Year for a chance to be the first person to burn incense at midnight - a tradition that is meant to give people the best luck and blessings.Scroll down for video                 All in for good luck: According to Chinese tradition, people burn joss sticks on the first day of the Lunar New Year to seek fortune.
