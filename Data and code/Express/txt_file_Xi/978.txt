     The sanctions include a ban on all exports of coal, aimed at cutting the rogue state’s annual export revenue of £230 billion by a third.North Korea sells most of its coal to China, which makes up 40 per cent of its exports.Donald Trump has urged China to put pressure on North Korea to stop its missile programmes and praised President Xi Jinping for banning all imports following the recent missile tests. GETTYCrowds gather in North Korea to watch report of missile launchThe South Korean leader also stressed the importance of keeping the door open to peace talks with the North if Pyongyang give up its nuclear programme.But North Korea has reiterated that it will not take its nuclear weapons off the negotiation table, pledging to stick to its policy of nuclear armament and economic development, known as the “byongjin policy”.
