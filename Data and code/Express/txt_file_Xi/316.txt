What is Davos 2017? Who is attending the World Economic Forum and what are the key points?
https://www.express.co.uk//news/world/755164/What-is-Davos-2017-World-Economic-Forum-Who-attending-list-key-points-themes
 09:56, Tue, Jan 17, 2017 
  GETTYTheresa May and Xi Jinping are among the 2,500 attending this year's DavosWhat is Davos 2017?Davos is a small town in the Swiss Alps that each January hosts the World Economic Forum (WEF) – a gathering of presidents, prime ministers, business leaders, economists and celebrities.The conference’s official calendar is full of panel discussions and speeches, while off-schedule there will also be private meetings between politicians, business leaders and senior bankers to discuss the key economic issues of the day.When is Davos 2017?This year’s WEF begins today and concludes on Friday January 20. Related articles   Matt Damon and Shakira attends World Economic Forum   China president makes first appearance at World Economic Forum What are the key themes of Davos 2017?The theme of this year’s meeting is responsive and responsible leadership.This has been broken down into five key areas: strengthening global collaboration, restoring economic growth, reforming capitalism, addressing the identity crisis which has caused the current populist revolution and preparing for the Fourth Industrial Revolution.Donald Trump’s imminent presidency, Brexit and China are set to be key topics of discussion.  GETTYThe event takes place in a small skiing town in the Swiss AlpsWho is attending Davos 2017?More than 2,500 people will attend this year’s WEF from 90 different countries.Theresa May will travel to Davos for the first time after she has made her landmark Brexit speech later today.She will be joined by Chancellor Philip Hammond, who will give a speech to outline his vision of the UK’s future economic relationship with the EU on Friday.Mayor of London Sadiq Khan will also attend and give a speech on the capital and Brexit.  GETTYUS Vice President Joe Biden spoke about his cancer initiative   Chinese president Xi Jinping is present and set to give an opening keynote speech today. He is the first Chinese leader to ever attend the conference.Beleaguered IMF director Christine Lagarde will participate in at least three panels on the world’s economic outlook, gender roles and fixing “the middle class crisis”.While the world is focused on Donald Trump’s upcoming inauguration, outgoing US Vice President Joe Biden has travelled to Switzerland to update attendees on the Cancer Moonshot initiative – his effort to beat the disease once and for all.
