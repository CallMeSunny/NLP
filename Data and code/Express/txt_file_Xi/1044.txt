Chinese mock Europe for 'being taken over by Muslims' - as more mosques are built in China
https://www.express.co.uk//news/world/710646/Chinese-mock-Europe-taken-over-Muslims-mosques-built-china
 00:00, Wed, Sep 14, 2016 
  GettyChinese people are showing a more anti-Muslim sentiment as more of the country becomes MuslimThe country's online community are increasingly spouting anti-Islamic sentiment amid the Government's religious restrictions on China's 20million-strong Muslim population.Parts of China's western region have seen a rise in the numbers of Islamic Chinese, causing friction the Government is keen to avoid as it bans media reporting on any state repression.And Xinjiang in the west, which is home to the predominantly Muslim Uighurs, is now the fifth highest source of Islamic State (ISIS) fighters behind three areas in Saudi Arabia and one in Tunisia, a report by Washington-based New America Foundation found in July. Related articles   Travel warning as Muslim protest mob attacks tourists in Turkey...   Air China mag warns tourists to use 'precautions' in London Many social media users have mocked the European Union's (EU) migrant policy, focusing on German Chancellor Angela Merkel's open-door agreement last year which saw 1.1m migrants come to Germany.On Weibo - China's version of Twitter - users are controversially saying the eurozone will soon become "Europestan".  GettyMuch of western China is MuslimFollowing suggestions from Western leaders China should accept its own quota of refugees from the Middle East, the Government outright refused.Chinese social media users supported the decision, with some incredibly even saying China should let the refugees stay in Europe so there are more terrorist attacks there.   As the Government attempts to censor Chinese social media users, they have started to use the term 'the Greens' to describe Muslims in derogatory terms, in reference to the colour being on many Muslim countries' flags.China has not been immune from Islamist attacks, with the Chinese embassy in Kyrgyzstan attacked by a suicide bomber at the end of August, injuring two security guards and three embassy staff.  GettyChina's Muslim communities have been continually ostracised by the Government  GettyMore mosques are being built in China, including in the southern city of ShenzhenChina and Kyrgyzstan blamed a Muslim Uighur suicide bomber on the attack, despite no group claiming responsibility.Beijing is struggling to control the racist rhetoric online while it is tiptoeing around the issue, with many Muslim ethnic minorities living in the west.Parts of the region now even have road signs in Arabic as well as Chinese and women wearing burkas or the hijab have become commonplace.
