Thrifty Theresa May wears same £250 designer necklace 21 TIMES since becoming PM

   The 60-year-old politician became Prime Minister back in July 2016 and is known for her eclectic taste in clothes.However, it appears Theresa May like to re-wear her garments, making her a very thrifty leader. May is a big fan of British brand Amanda Wakeley - a costly brand.  A fur black coat on the website costs £4,695, which a long shearing gilet costs £1,895.She wore a navy coat by designer Amanda Wakeley with a bold neon yellow stripe along the bottom for her first day at Downing Street in July 2016.     Back in the UK the next week, May walked with Martek Managing Director Derek Galloway and Croydon Central MP Gavin Barwell during a visit to Martinek joinery factory on August 3.She wore the jewellery to meet Chinese President Xi Jinping at the West Lake State House on September 5, 2016 in Hangzhou, China. Two days later the Prime Minister left Number 10 Downing Street in the jewellery for Prime Minister's Questions in the House of Commons, on September 7.May met his Highness Sheikh Tamim bin Hamad al Thani, The Emir of Qatar outside 10 Downing Street on September 15 in the necklace and bracelet, and the Prime Minister of Pakistan, Nawaz Sharif in New York in the look on September 19. GETTYTheresa May in Nottingham: May is a big fan of British brand Amanda WakeleyIn an interview with US Vogue in March 2017, May said: "Look, throughout my political career, people have commented on what I wear."That’s just something that happens, and you accept that.
