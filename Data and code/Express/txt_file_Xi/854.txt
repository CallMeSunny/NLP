GBP v NZD: Pound hits best levels of 2017 against New Zealand Dollar
https://www.express.co.uk//finance/city/788610/gbp-nzd-exchange-rate-pound-best-levels-2017-new-zealand-dollar
 10:45, Thu, Apr 6, 2017 
  GETTYThe GBP/NZD exchange rate reached NZ$1.795 – it’s highest levels of the year so farWith the UK’s latest service sector PMI showing surprising strength, the pound spent the day climbing against all its major currency rivals. GBP/EUR pushed above €1.170 while GBP/USD tested $1.250.The pound also racked up notable gains against the Australian dollar, but it was against the New Zealand dollar that the currency’s performance was most impressive.Although this week’s dairy auction revealed an increase in the price of Australia’s key commodity, the GBP/NZD exchange rate was still able to surge from NZ$1.782 to NZ$1.795 – it’s highest levels of the year so far.     "The Caixin China Composite Output Index was 52.1 in March, down 0.5 points from February and the worst reading in half a year. "The Chinese economy continued to expand in March, but growth in both manufacturing and services slowed. "Weaker increases in new business have clouded the economic outlook, and investors should watch closely for signs of a turning point in the second quarter.”The Australian dollar also weakened in the wake of the Chinese data, giving GBP/AUD the opportunity to climb by a further 0.3 per cent as European markets opened.New Zealand’s economic calendar is looking pretty bare for the rest of the week, but tomorrow’s US news has the potential to spark volatility in both NZD and AUD exchange rates before the weekend.  GETTYWorth following the meeting between US President Donald Trump and Chinese president Xi JinpingIf the US non-farm payrolls report shows a strong increase in job growth it would support the argument in favour of the Federal Reserve increasing interest rates more than three times in 2017.However, the change in non-farm payrolls figure is expected to come in at 177k in March – down from 235k in February.An on or below forecast result may encourage the Fed to continue with its current plans for just two more rate adjustments this year, increasing demand for higher-risk currencies like the New Zealand dollar and Australian dollar in the process.Other news worth following includes the meeting between US President Donald Trump and Chinese president Xi Jinping. If the two clash over Trump’s plans for US trade, the GBP/NZD exchange rate could give up some of this week’s gains.
