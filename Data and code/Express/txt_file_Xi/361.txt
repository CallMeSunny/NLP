   The US President told reporters aboard Air Force One on the way to Florida for his meeting with his Chinese counterpart Xi Jinping that he was prepared to act unilaterally if China would not step up and take action to help.When asked whether he would be willing act alone on North Korea, Mr Trump said: "Certainly I would be." He then added: "But I think China will want to be stepping up."  GettyUS President Donald Trump has said he is prepared to act unilaterally over North KoreaThe US has indicated that it would want China to be involved with the Secretary of State Rex Tillerson saying earlier that China could be part of a new strategy to curb North Korea’s nuclear programme.Beijing says its influence is limited and that it is doing all it can but that it is up to the United States to find a way back to talks with North Korea.Mr Trump consulted with Japanese Prime Minister Shinzo Abe yesterday, who said he and the President agreed by phone that North Korea's latest ballistic missile launch was "a dangerous provocation and a serious threat."
