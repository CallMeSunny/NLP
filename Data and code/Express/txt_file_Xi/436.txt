'Golden Era' returns: Theresa May leads charge for Brexit trade deal with China
https://www.express.co.uk//news/politics/911589/brexit-theresa-may-britain-china-trade-deal-golden-era-liam-fox-latest
 00:01, Tue, Jan 30, 2018 
   The Prime Minister will be joined by 50 business leaders from all sectors of the UK economy on her RAF Voyager aircraft for the three-day visit to Wuhan, Beijing and Shanghai.She will also be accompanied by International Trade Secretary Liam Fox and her husband Philip May.Face-to-face talks with both Chinese President Xi Jinping and premier Li Keqiang are scheduled to discuss opportunities for increasing the trade links between Britain and China, worth an estimated £60billion last year.Ahead of the trip, Mrs May said last night: “The UK and China are both global powers with a global outlook and I am committed to deepening our strong and vital partnership with what is one of the world’s fastest growing economies. GETTYTrade between the UK and China is at record levels and has grown over 60% since 2010“My visit will intensify the 'Golden Era' in UK-China relations. The depth of our relationship means we can have frank discussions on all issues.”Official figures show that trade between the UK and China is at record levels and has grown by over 60% since 2010.Mrs May will seek to develop the "Golden Era" in British-Chinese relations declared by President Xi during his state visit to London two years ago.The Prime Minister is also expected to discuss the missile threat from North Korea and environmental issues during the visit.  GETTYMrs May will be accompanied by her husband Philip, and it will be his first official overseas tripBusiness leaders joining the Prime Minister's trade mission are expected to include Confederation of British Industry director-general Carolyn Fairbairn, Jaguar Land Rover chief executive Ralf Speth, Norton Motorcycles boss Robert McFarlane and Karen Betts of the Scottish Whiskey Association.Dr Fox said: “Trade between our two countries is already worth more than £59billion and UK exports to China increased by over 25% last year.“Our relationship is now more important than ever, as we look to form new trading bonds with the biggest growing markets around the world. “Indeed, China’s middle class is expected to number 600 million by 2020 - greater than the current entire population of the EU - presenting unrivalled opportunities for UK business.
