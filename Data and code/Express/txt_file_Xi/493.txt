   North Korea’s despotic leader Kim Jong-un is prepared to meet with Donald Trump, South Korea announced yesterday, setting the stage for a historic showdown.And today Chinese president Xi Jinping announced he supported the meeting in a phone call with Mr Trump.President Xi told Mr Trump he appreciates his desire to resolve the North Korea issue politically rather than militarily.Chinese state media said the pair talked by phone earlier today, hours after it was revealed Kim will meet Mr Trump face-to-ace.     The state-run news agency said Mr Xi told Mr Trump he hopes all sides can show goodwill and avoid doing anything that may affect or interfere with the improving situation on the Korean peninsula.And he also said he hoped China and the US would continue to work together economically.Today the UN also spoke out in support of the talks.   Secretary-General Antonio Guterres’s spokesman said he is encouraged by the planned meeting.He said: “He commends the leadership and vision of all concerned and reiterates his support for all efforts towards peaceful denuclearization of the Korean peninsula in accordance with relevant Security Council resolutions.”Yesterday South Korea's National Security Office head Chung Eui-yong said Mr Trump had accepted an invitation by Kim for a face-to-face meeting.
