European Central Bank President Mario Draghi fell out of the top 10 to number 11.Shinzo Abe, the Japanese prime minister, saw the biggest jump in power, as he moved up 22 spots to sit at number 49.And it seems money yields power with 28 of the 73 people on the list holding billionaire status.Just nine of the people listed are women. Behind Ms Merkel, Janet Yellen, chair of the US Federal Reserve, equivalent to the Bank of England, is the highest ranking woman.Facebook chief executive Mark Zuckerberg ranks at number 19 and the 31-year-old is the youngest on the list.North Korea Supreme Leader Kim Jong-un, ranked at 46 and aged 31, is also one of the youngest on the list.The list is selected based on the amount of money control, the number of people they impact, their total spheres of influence, and how actively they wield their power.
