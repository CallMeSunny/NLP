Britain’s OPEN for business: Bullish May to pitch leaders at first G20 meeting post-Brexit
https://www.express.co.uk//news/politics/706787/Britainbusiness-May-G20-meeting-post-Brexit
 00:01, Sat, Sep 3, 2016 
   The future of trading relationships is the Prime Minister’s top priority as she attends her first major summit since taking office.It will come up in bilateral discussions with President Obama, the Chinese President Xi Jinping and Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi as well as Mrs May being give a leading slot to discuss world trade at the first session of the summit.After a week where Mrs May declared that she intends to press ahead with Brexit as fast as possible and not allow MPs a chance to vote it down or hold a second referendum, the Prime Minister intends to use her trip to China to start outlining her vision of Britain as a leading free trade nation.Her confidence in selling Britain as a trading partner comes amid reports that the Transatlantic Trade and Investment Partnership (TTIP) deal between the EU and US is on the verge of collapse, meaning that Brexit is Britain’s best chance to have a trade deal with America. It is an issue that was discussed at Cabinet at Chequers on Wednesday the idea of what what does our new place in the world look like after we have left the EU? What are those relations like?“We have already had discussions with a number of countries where they are interested in looking at what that relationship will be like and I think this will be ahn opportunity to start some conversations.”It is expected that Mrs May will urge Mr Obama to rethink his claim that Britain would be “at the back of a queue” for a trade deal if it voted for Brexit with his country’s deal with the EU now on the verge of collapse.She is also set to remind him that the UK is America’s closest ally and already a successful trading partner.     The briefing comes as Foreign Secretary Boris Johnson has laid out a positive vision of a new trading relationship with the EU which does not include free movement across British borders.He said that Britain is seeking a "strong new European partnership" with the remaining 27 members of the EU after Brexit.Mr Johnson said he wants the UK to continue to work with the EU on issues like migration following its withdrawal.He was speaking in Vienna after talks with his Austrian counterpart Sebastian Kurz ahead of an informal gathering of EU foreign ministers which they were both attending later in Slovakian capital Bratislava.
