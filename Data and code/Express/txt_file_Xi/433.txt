Battle to save the elephants: America and China join forces to fight illegal ivory racket
https://www.express.co.uk//news/nature/608198/America-China-elephant-hunting-ivory-trade

  GETTYBarack Obama and Xi Jinping during their official meetingUS President Barack Obama and his Chinese counterpart Xi Jinping have agreed to take swift action to help save Africa’s threatened elephants as well as other iconic animals being slaughtered for the wildlife blackmarket.More than 35,000 elephants are slaughtered every year to fuel the demand for ivory, with China the epicentre of the illegal trade, largely down to way ivory is seen as a symbol of affluence among the country’s noveau riche.Small ivory elephants are a good fortune symbol across the Far East, although they are far from lucky for the huge numbers of elephants that are trapped and snared to feed the illicit markets.     Azzedine Downes, chief executive of the International Fund for Animal Welfare (IFAW), said: “Mr Xi has today delivered a tremendous victory in the battle to save elephants."China has slammed the door in the face of all those who are profiting from the slaughter of elephants."As the world’s largest market for legal and illegal ivory, this ban will save the lives of tens of thousands of elephants."In London, IFAW’s UK director Philip Mansbridge added: "It is ground-breaking that global powers are taking the leadership role in the fight to save elephants. now building to be the generation that saves the elephant."The African Wildlife Foundation also welcomed the public commitment from the world’s two largest economies to work together to bring an end to the elephant poaching crisis.Its chief executive Dr Patrick Bergin said: “President Obama and President Xi are sending a clear message that they intend to throw the weight of their countries behind the elephant crisis.“If these commitments translate into meaningful cooperation and action by these geopolitical giants on tackling poaching and wildlife trafficking, the future will be bright for Africa’s giants.”  GETTYThe two world leaders making their agreement  GETTYTogether the leaders hope to tackle the illegal ivory trade   Details of the Sino-American agreement were revealed in a White House fact sheet which revealed:• The United States and China, recognising the importance and urgency of combating wildlife trafficking, commit to take positive measures to address this global challenge.• The United States and China commit to enact nearly complete bans on ivory import and export, including significant and timely restrictions on the import of ivory as hunting trophies, and to take significant and timely steps to halt the domestic commercial trade of ivory.• The two sides decided to further cooperate in joint training, technical exchanges, information sharing, and public education on combating wildlife trafficking, and enhance international law enforcement cooperation in this field.• The United States and China decided to cooperate with other nations in a comprehensive effort to combat wildlife trafficking.
