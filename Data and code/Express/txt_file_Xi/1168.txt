  GETTYMelania Trump: A salami has been brought out using the First Lady's titleMelania Trump might be the First Lady of the United States but she grew up in the quiet town of Sevnica, Slovenia. Although now officially an American citizen - as of 2006 - the stunning 46-year-old is proud of her roots and it would seem her hometown is proud of her too. The tourist trade is cashing in on their famous counterpart, putting her First Lady title on all manner of items to shift them to the abundance of tourists now visiting the town. From First Lady salami to a tipple of First Lady wine - it's all going down in Sevnica.  Related articles   Melania Trump shows her maternal side in a look to meet children   Melania Trump - did YOU spot the subtle alterations to THIS red dress?   GETTYMelania Trump: The First Lady's home town has brought out wine using her title  GETTYMelania Trump: This cake is called The Melania and sells for $3The number of American visitors to Slovenia jumped by 11 percent to 80,000 between January and October last year compared to the same period in 2015, according to the Slovenian Tourism Board. Sevnica, nestled in the foothills of the Alps, is famed for its wine tasting, embracing outdoor sports and draws in tourists keen to see the river Sava threading through the area. A pocket-guide to Sevnica reads: "…it would be easy to write off Sevnica as a single-faceted destination, one that makes headlines only for its status as Melania Trump's hometown. "However, while there are not many other classic sights in the town centre, the municipality is full of worthwhile things to see and do - especially for those interested in wine tasting."  GETTYMelania Trump: The First Lady wore this stunning red dress to a formal dinner last week   The First Lady wine on sale at the town’s 900-year-old castle will set tourists back a meagre €27.90 a bottle. And a local bakery was selling the Melania cake from December '16 - cheap at the price of $3 a slice. Sevnica's mayor, Srečko Ocvirk, spoke to press after Trump was announced as the winner of the elections last year. He said: "Every American election is important, but this is very special for us"[The election] created a sense of excitement for us in Sevnica and we have been following developments with interest."And Mirjana Jelančič, Melania’s childhood friend, told the local Siol.net news site: "All that they said about her [and] wrote about her was controversial, insulting and that is not the Melania I know because she was a very good girl, wonderful friend, excellent human being and always well intended.
