  GETTYCould iPhone sales be blocked in this major Apple market?iPhone users across the world could be set to miss out on new devices, according to new warnings.Customers in one of Apple's biggest markets may face long delays or even a total ban of the company's devices such as the iPhone 7, experts have said.The move could lead to the company suffering a major decline in fortunes in China.  GETTYDonald Trump will take over as President in JanuaryState-run newspaper Global Times has warned that the Chinese government is set to respond with "countermeasures" if President-elect Donald Trump follows through on past promises.Trump pledged to introduce a 45 per cent tariff on Chinese exports to the US during his campaign as part of a bid to encourage US industry.At a rally in June, Trump told an audience that the US was already in, "a trade war. And we’re losing, badly."   The Global Times editorial said that if this war materalises, China will take, "a tit-for-tat approach"."A batch of Boeing orders will be replaced by Airbus. US auto and iPhone sales in China will suffer a setback, and US soybean and maize imports will be halted. China can also limit the number of Chinese student studying in the US."It added that, "a number of US industries will be impaired," if Trump destroys US-China relations.
