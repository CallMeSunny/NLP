China warns of PROFOUND change as US trade war sees economic growth drop to 30 year low
https://www.express.co.uk//news/world/1096366/china-v-usa-trade-war-national-people-s-congress-beijing-li-keqiang
 11:44, Wed, Mar 6, 2019 
   Premier Li Keqiang told the opening of the annual National People’s Congress in Beijing’s Great Hall that the country now faced a “complicated terrain of increasing dilemmas”. Relations with the US remain tense with military sabre-rattling across the disputed South China Sea, allegations of spying against Chinese telecoms giant Huawei and continuing scrutiny of Beijing's record on human rights doing little to help negotiations aimed at resolving the stand-off over trade between the world's two biggest economies. Delegates arrive for congress in traditonal regional costume (Image: EPA)  Chinese Premier Li Keqiang with President Xi Jinping (Image: EPA)The tax cuts are much more aggressive than those delivered last year and include reductions aimed at supporting the manufacturing, transport and construction sectors.China's GDP last year expanded at its slowest pace since 1990 due to the trade war and Beijing's crackdown on financial risks, which raised corporate borrowing costs and hurt investment.A longer-term campaign to curb polluting and low-value industries also slowed China's vast manufacturing sector.China has ramped up the propaganda in the run-up to parliament, with state media releasing English-language videos and even a rap song praising the event as an exercise in Chinese democracy, though the legislature never rejects laws and its members are chosen for their loyalty to the Communist Party. At the same time, it will cut the social security fees paid by companies.  More Communist Party members arrive for the annual gathering in Beijing (Image: EPA)Addressing one of the biggest challenges China's economy faces, the government's report to parliament said it would continue to promote trade negotiations with the US and was committed to safeguarding economic globalisation and free trade.US Secretary of State Mike Pompeo said he thought both nations were "on the cusp" of a deal to end their trade war.
